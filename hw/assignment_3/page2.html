SAYGE is a soul-pop trio whose tight, eclectic harmonies and vintage groove create a new contemporary sound with covers and original tunes. The Miami-based group formed in 2017 and the three unique voices have been blending to make refreshing, sophisticated music ever since. Similar to their namesake herb, SAYGE’s music exudes organic flavor far beyond its years that pairs perfectly with a nice glass of cabernet.

​Matt Gagnon has spent most of his life in the classical music world, where he has won State-Wide and National awards for his singing, as well as for playing piano and cello. He made the switch to the contemporary world upon beginning his college career at the University of Miami, where he currently studies Music Business and Contemporary Songwriting. He has now released several original songs on SoundCloud, and was featured as a soloist on BisCaydence’s latest a cappella album, titled Against the Current. He hopes to be releasing his first EP as a solo artist by December. ​

Carly Mindel is a Pop/EDM singer and songwriter. At the start of her career, Carly engaged in voiceover commercials for Elias Arts where she composed melodies and recorded vocals for well-known commercial advertisements. Additionally, Carly has co-written with industry recognized DJ’s and producers and is featured on several artist releases. In 2016, Carly wrote, recorded and released her single “Feel It Too” from her latest Pop/EDM album which can be found on iTunes, Spotify and other music services. Most recently, Carly performed at the 60th Annual Grammy Awards at Madison Square Garden alongside Logic, Alessia Cara and Khalid. ​

Jamie Robinson has been well versed in many musical genres throughout her career. She grew up singing in choir and vocal jazz ensembles and starring in musical theater productions. Jamie has shared the Broadway stage with theater greats Norm Lewis, Lea Salonga, and Brian D’Arcy James. She has also worked as a sound engineer with Bruce Hornsby, DJ Mark Farina, and Ben Folds. Most recently, Jamie has been writing contemporary music. Her vast musical background has led her to create songs that smartly blend jazz and musical theater styles while telling stories of her real life experiencess.
